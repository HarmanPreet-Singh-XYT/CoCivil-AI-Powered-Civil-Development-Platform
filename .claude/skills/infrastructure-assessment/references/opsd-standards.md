# Ontario Provincial Standard Drawings — Pipes, Manholes, and Underground Infrastructure

The Ontario Provincial Standard Drawings (OPSD) provide standardized construction details adopted by most Ontario municipalities. They are published by the Ontario Provincial Standards Committee and updated periodically. OPSD drawings are referenced in OPSS specifications and municipal development standards.

---

## 1. Storm Sewer Pipes — OPSD 701 Series

### OPSD 701.010 — Concrete Storm Sewer Pipe

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Material | Reinforced concrete pipe (RCP) to CSA A257 |
| Diameters | 250 mm to 3000 mm |
| Joint type | Bell and spigot with rubber gasket (CSA A257.3) |
| Minimum class | Class 3 (for standard installations) |
| Wall thickness | Per CSA A257 tables — varies by diameter and class |
| Interior finish | Smooth, free of cracks exceeding hairline |

### OPSD 701.020 — PVC Storm Sewer Pipe

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Material | PVC to CSA B182.2 (solid wall) or CSA B182.4 (profile wall) |
| Diameters | 100 mm to 600 mm (solid wall), up to 900 mm (profile wall) |
| Stiffness class | Minimum 320 kPa (PS 46) for gravity applications |
| Joint type | Bell and spigot with elastomeric gasket |
| UV protection | Must be protected from sunlight during storage |

### OPSD 701.030 — HDPE Storm Sewer Pipe

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Material | High-density polyethylene to CSA B182.6 or CSA B182.8 |
| Diameters | 100 mm to 900 mm |
| Stiffness class | Minimum 320 kPa |
| Joint type | Bell and spigot with gasket, or electrofusion/butt fusion |
| Applications | Preferred for corrosive soil conditions and trenchless installations |

### OPSD 701.040 — Corrugated Steel Pipe (CSP)

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Material | Galvanized corrugated steel to CSA G401 |
| Diameters | 300 mm to 3600 mm |
| Gauge | 1.6 mm to 4.0 mm depending on diameter and fill height |
| Coatings | Galvanized standard; polymer laminate or bituminous coating for corrosive conditions |
| Service life | 25-50 years (highly dependent on soil and water pH) |
| Joints | Corrugated band couplers with gaskets |

**Assessment note**: CSP has the shortest expected service life of common pipe materials. Invert deterioration and perforation are the most common failure modes. Assess bottom-of-pipe condition first.

## 2. Sanitary Sewer Pipes — OPSD 702 Series

### OPSD 702.010 — PVC Sanitary Sewer Pipe

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Material | PVC to CSA B182.2 (solid wall, SDR 35) |
| Diameters | 150 mm to 375 mm (residential laterals and local collectors) |
| Stiffness class | SDR 35, minimum pipe stiffness 320 kPa |
| Joint type | Bell and spigot with elastomeric gasket |
| Minimum grade | Per municipality — typically 1.0% for 200mm, 0.67% for 250mm |
| Infiltration limit | Per OPSS 410 leakage testing |

### OPSD 702.020 — Concrete Sanitary Sewer Pipe

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Material | Reinforced concrete to CSA A257 |
| Diameters | 250 mm to 3000 mm (trunk sewers) |
| Interior lining | Polyethylene or PVC liner required for H2S protection in trunk sewers |
| Joint type | Rubber gasket bell and spigot |
| Class | Minimum Class 3, Class 5 for deep installations |

**Assessment note**: Concrete sanitary sewers in service 40+ years should be inspected for crown deterioration from hydrogen sulfide (H2S) attack. Crown loss exceeding 10% of wall thickness is a structural concern.

## 3. Watermain Pipes — OPSD 704 Series

### OPSD 704.010 — Ductile Iron Watermain

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Material | Ductile iron to AWWA C151/A21.51 |
| Diameters | 100 mm to 1500 mm |
| Pressure class | Minimum Class 350 (2400 kPa) |
| Lining | Cement mortar lining to AWWA C104/A21.4 |
| External protection | Polyethylene encasement (AWWA C105/A21.5) in corrosive soils |
| Joint type | Push-on (Tyton) or mechanical joint |
| Design life | 75-100+ years |

### OPSD 704.020 — PVC Watermain

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Material | PVC to AWWA C900 (DR 18 or DR 25) |
| Diameters | 100 mm to 300 mm |
| Pressure class | Class 150 (1035 kPa) for DR 18 |
| Joint type | Push-on with elastomeric gasket |
| Tracer wire | Required — watermain is non-metallic |
| Design life | 75-100 years |

## 4. Manholes — OPSD 704 Series (Manholes)

### OPSD 704.010 — Standard Precast Concrete Manhole

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Material | Precast reinforced concrete to CSA A257.4 |
| Internal diameter | 1200 mm standard (900 mm for shallow, 1500 mm+ for large trunk sewers) |
| Barrel sections | Minimum 150 mm wall thickness |
| Base | Precast or cast-in-place with formed channels |
| Frame and cover | Per OPSD 401.010 — heavy duty for roadway, light duty for non-traffic areas |
| Steps | Polypropylene steps at 400 mm centres (OPSD 704.011) or ladder |
| Benching | Minimum 25 mm/m slope from wall to channel invert |

### Manhole Spacing

| Pipe Diameter | Maximum Spacing |
|---------------|----------------|
| Up to 675 mm | 120 m |
| 750 mm to 1050 mm | 150 m |
| 1200 mm and larger | 180 m |

**Assessment note**: Manhole condition assessment should check frame seating, barrel joint separation, invert channel integrity, step/ladder condition, and infiltration at joints. H2S attack on concrete manholes is assessed at the crown and above the flow line.

## 6. Bedding and Cover — OPSD 802 Series

### OPSD 802.010 — Bedding and Cover for Rigid Pipes

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Bedding class | Class B standard (granular bedding, 100 mm minimum under pipe) |
| Bedding material | Granular A or B (OPSS 1010) |
| Minimum cover — under roadway | 1.2 m from top of pipe to finished road surface |
| Minimum cover — outside roadway | 0.6 m from top of pipe |
| Maximum cover | Per pipe class — structural design required beyond standard tables |

### OPSD 802.030 — Bedding and Cover for Flexible Pipes

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Bedding class | Class B (granular bedding and initial backfill to 300 mm above pipe) |
| Compaction | 95% Standard Proctor in haunch zone |
| Minimum cover — under roadway | 0.9 m (flexible pipe deforms under load — reduced cover is acceptable) |
| Embedment | Granular material compacted in 150 mm lifts in the haunch zone |

**Assessment note**: Flexible pipe failures often originate from inadequate haunch compaction during installation, causing excessive vertical deflection. Deflection exceeding 5% of pipe diameter indicates a significant installation or loading problem.

## 7. Trenchless Installation — OPSD 803 Series

### Pipe Bursting

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Host pipe | Existing pipe to be fractured in place |
| New pipe | HDPE or PVC — must be equal or larger diameter |
| Minimum depth | No OPSD minimum, but practical minimum 1.5 m for equipment |
| Lateral reconnection | Required within 2 weeks of burst |

### Horizontal Directional Drilling (HDD)

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Pipe material | HDPE (fused) preferred, PVC (restrained joint) acceptable |
| Minimum depth | 1.5 m below finished grade, deeper at crossings |
| Crossing clearance | Minimum 300 mm vertical separation from other utilities |

## 8. Common Deficiency Types for Assessment

### Pipe Deficiencies (PACP Coding Reference)

| Code | Deficiency | Severity Indicators |
|------|-----------|---------------------|
| Crack | Longitudinal, circumferential, or spiral | Width: hairline (<1mm) = minor; 1-3mm = moderate; >3mm = severe |
| Fracture | Crack with visible displacement | Any displacement = moderate to severe |
| Broken | Pipe pieces visible or missing | Severe to critical |
| Deformation | Vertical deflection of flexible pipe | <5% = minor; 5-10% = moderate; >10% = severe |
| Joint offset | Misalignment at pipe joint | <10mm = minor; 10-25mm = moderate; >25mm = severe |
| Infiltration | Groundwater entering pipe | Weeper = minor; runner = moderate; gusher = severe |
| Root intrusion | Roots through joints or cracks | Fine roots = minor; medium ball = moderate; full blockage = severe |
| Corrosion | Material loss from chemical attack | Surface = minor; >25% wall loss = severe |

### Manhole Deficiencies

| Deficiency | Severity Indicators |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Frame settlement | <25mm = minor; 25-50mm = moderate; >50mm = severe |
| Barrel joint separation | <10mm = minor; 10-25mm = moderate; >25mm = severe |
| Infiltration | Damp = minor; running = moderate; flowing = severe |
| Invert deterioration | Surface wear = minor; channel loss = moderate; through-wall = severe |
| Step/ladder failure | Loose = moderate; missing or broken = severe |
