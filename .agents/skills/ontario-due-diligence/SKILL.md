## 16. Consolidated Due Diligence Report

### What It Is
A comprehensive pre-acquisition report for investors and developers that combines zoning analysis, development potential, financial feasibility, and risk assessment into a single document.

### Why It Matters
- Investors need this **before purchasing property** to understand what can be built
- Lenders may require this for construction financing
- Saves weeks of separate research

### How to Build It

```
CONSOLIDATED DUE DILIGENCE REPORT

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
- Property address and description
- Current use and condition
- Development potential (number of units, GFA, height)
- Estimated project cost
- Key risks and opportunities
- GO / CAUTION / NO-GO recommendation

SECTION 1: PROPERTY INFORMATION
- Legal description, PIN, lot dimensions
- Current assessed value (MPAC)
- Title search summary (encumbrances, easements, rights-of-way)
- Current tax amount

SECTION 2: PLANNING FRAMEWORK
- Official Plan designation and applicable policies
- Zoning category and development standards
- Secondary Plan (if applicable)
- Any active municipal studies or policy reviews affecting the area

SECTION 3: DEVELOPMENT POTENTIAL
- Scenario A: As-of-right development (what can be built today)
- Scenario B: Minor variance development (what can be built
  with variances)
- Scenario C: Rezoning development (maximum potential with
  by-law amendment)
- For each scenario: unit count, GFA, height, parking

SECTION 4: COMPLIANCE MATRIX
[From Section 2 of this guide — for each scenario]

SECTION 5: APPROVAL PATHWAY
[From Section 17 of this guide]

SECTION 6: REQUIRED STUDIES AND ESTIMATED COSTS
[From Section 15 of this guide]

SECTION 7: FINANCIAL FEASIBILITY
[From Section 6 of this guide — for each scenario]

SECTION 8: RISK ASSESSMENT

|------|-----------|--------|------------|
```
