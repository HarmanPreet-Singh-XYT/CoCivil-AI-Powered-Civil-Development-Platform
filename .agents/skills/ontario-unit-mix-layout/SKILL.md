## 8. Unit Mix and Layout Summary

### What It Is
A breakdown of the proposed residential units by type, size, bedroom count, and accessibility compliance.

### Why It Matters
- Official Plans require a **mix of unit types and sizes** to accommodate diverse households
- Growth Plan requires municipalities to plan for a range of housing options
- Some municipalities have minimum unit size requirements or family-sized unit policies
- Accessibility requirements under the Ontario Building Code and AODA

### Governing Law
- **Provincial Planning Statement 2024, Section 2.4** — Range and mix of housing
- **Growth Plan, Section 2.2.6** — Diverse housing options
- **Official Plan housing policies** (e.g., Toronto OP Section 3.2.1)
- **Growing Up Guidelines (Toronto)** — Requirements for larger units suitable for families
- **Ontario Building Code, O. Reg. 332/12, Section 3.8** — Barrier-free design
- **AODA** — Accessibility standards
